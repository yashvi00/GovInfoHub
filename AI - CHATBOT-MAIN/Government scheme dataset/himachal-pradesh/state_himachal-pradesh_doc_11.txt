
Himachal Pradesh govt. has decided to implement HP Ujjwala Grahini Suvidha Yojana (GSY) in the cabinet meeting held on 8 January 2019. Himachal Grihini Suvidha Yojana application form is available in PDF format at the official website. People can download E-KYC form and application form pdf of Grahani Suvidha Yojna to avail domestic LPG gas connections.
Grihini Suvidha Yojana in Himachal Pradesh is going to result in women empowerment and will also help in creating a pollution free environment in the state. HP govt. will implement this Grahini Suvidha Yojana (HGSY) with an outlay of Rs. 12 crore.
Previously, the state govt. has announced this Grihini Sividha Yojna in the annual Himachal Pradesh Budget 2018-19.

Himachal Grihini Suvidha Yojana Application Form Download
Below is the complete procedure to download the Himachal Grihini Suvidha Yojana Form PDF:-

Firstly visit the website http://admis.hp.nic.in/ehimapurti/downloads.htm
At the homepage, click at the “Himachal Grihini Suvidha Yojana Form” link.

Afterwards, the E-KYC form and application form for HP Grahani Suvidha Yojana will appear on the 1st & 2nd page in the pdf file. E-KYC form will appear as shown below:-
Himachal Grihini Suvidha Yojana E-KYC Form Download
Moreover, Himachal Grihini Suvidha Yojana Application Form will appear as shown below:-
Himachal Grihini Suvidha Yojana Application Form
Download the PDF file, fill both the E-KYC & application forms for Grahini Suvidha Yojana and submit it too the concerned authorities to avail domestic LPG gas connections.

Himachal Grahini Suvidha Yojana Details
The highlights of Himachal Pradesh Grahani Suvidha Yojna are as follows:-

HP govt. will provide security amount for domestic Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) connection and a gas stove to the poor families.
All families in the state who does not possess the LPG Gas Connections and are not covered under Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) will get covered under HP Grahani Suvidha Scheme 2019.
This scheme aims to provide LPG Gas connection facility to all the poor families for at-least 2 years.
