
Jharkhand govt. is going to launch Mukhyamantri Krishi Aashirwad Yojana 2019 – 2020 for farmers. Under this investment support scheme, govt. will provide financial assistance of Rs. 5,000 per acre for kharif crops every year. This Mukhyamantri Krishi Ashirwad Yojana 2019 in Jharkhand is going to benefit around 22 lakh 76 thousand small and marginal farmers in the state.
In order to successfully implement Jharkhand Mukhyamantri Krishi Aashirwad Yojana, govt. will spend Rs. 2,250 crore. The main focus of this Mukhyamantri Krishi Ashirwad Scheme 2019-20 is to raise the agricultural production, eliminate agarian crisis and to overcome farmers distress.
It is essential to note that only those small and marginal farmers whose total land holding is less than 5 acres can avail the scheme benefits. This scheme will realize the PM Modi’s vision of Doubling Farmers Income by 2022.

Jharkhand Mukhyamantri Krishi Ashirwad Yojana 2019
Under Mukhyamantri Krishi Aashirwad Yojana, govt. will provide Rs. 5,000 per acre per annum to farmers for Kharif crops. All the farmers whose land holdings are less than 1 acre would also get minimum Rs. 5,000 every year. This assistance amount would be given through cheques under Jharkhand Mukhyamantri Krishi Ashirwad Yojana 2019. Around 22.76 lakh small and marginal farmers would be benefited through this scheme. 
Govt. will make a budgetary provision of Rs. 2250 crore for this Jharkhand Mukhyamantri Krishi Aashirwad Yojana 2019-20. Around 45 lakh acres agricultural land would be covered under this Krishi Ashirwad Scheme. Now the farmers will not have to depend on any banks or money lenders to buy seeds (beej), compost (khaad) and to make other agricultural investment.

Jaharkhand Mukhyamantri Krishi Ashirwad Yojana 2019 is an investment support scheme which will prevent farmers from taking loans as it will make them self dependent. The state govt. is consistently working to improve the financial conditions of farmers. Govt. is making loans available for farmers @0% interest rate. Moreover, govt. is paying Rs. 66 crore as premium for fasal bima (crop insurance) of 14.85 lakh farmers.
