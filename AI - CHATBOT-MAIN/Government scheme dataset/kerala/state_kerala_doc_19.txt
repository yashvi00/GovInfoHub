
Kerala govt. has launched Muttathe Mulla Loan Scheme. Under this scheme, the state govt. will provide loans at lower interest rate to rural families especially women. Now, women will not have have to take loans from private dealers and micro-finance companies whose interest rate varies between 24% to 30% per annum (2% to 2.5% per month). There is also a provision of loan repayment on weekly basis. 
This scheme will benefit common people who are being exploited by the private financers charging higher interest rates from poor people. This is an ambitious micro finance programme in the co-operative sector to help people.
Mannarkkad Rural Service Cooperative Bank has organized the programme for inauguration of this loan scheme. In the first phase, govt. will implement this scheme in Palakkad district as a pilot project.

Women will get liberated from the from the vicious circle of interest on loans from blade mafia and also from other micro finance companies. Govt. will implement this scheme with the help of Kudumbashree. Govt. will provide loans upto Rs. 10 lakh to these Kudumbashree units at 7% interest rate p.a. The government is taking several steps to regulate loan sharks through a joint initiative of cooperative banks and Kudumbashree.
Each woman can apply for a loan amount of Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 25,000. They can also take loans to make payment of loans taken from other dealers / micro finance companies and to close old loans. Such loans can be settled on a One-Time basis for which the interest rate is 12% per annum. This accounts to interest rate of 1% per month i.e women will have to only pay Rs. 1 as interest for a loan amount of Rs 100 for one month.

This scheme will disburse loans at lower interest rates and will ease the process on how to make repayment on weekly basis. There are various loans category and facility for loans which can be repaid in 10 weeks were also available. 
Implementation of Muttathe Mulla Loan Scheme
First 3 units of Kudumbashree will implement this scheme in every ward. Govt. will select Kudumbashree units on the basis of their efficiency in working and credibility. The members of these Kudumbashree units will reach the houses of the people who are in need of such loans and disburse it to them. 
Moreover, these members will also visit the houses of the loan beneficiaries on a weekly basis for loan repayment. As per the loan demand, primary cooperative societies will disburse loans upto Rs. 10 lakh to Kudumbashree units at around 7% interest rate. These units can give loans to women in the neighbourhood neetwork at 12% per annum interest rate. The first phase will start from Palakkad district on 29 June 2018.
